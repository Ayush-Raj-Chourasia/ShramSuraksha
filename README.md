## 💡 The Problem

India's **15+ million platform-based gig delivery workers** — riding for **Zomato, Swiggy, Zepto, Amazon, Dunzo, and others** — are the backbone of our fast-paced digital economy. Yet they remain completely unprotected against external shocks.

### The Crisis
> **External disruptions such as extreme weather, severe air pollution, natural disasters, and civic unrest can reduce a gig worker's monthly income by 20–30%.**

**Real Impact:**
- A Zomato delivery partner earning ₹60,000/month in summer suddenly loses ₹15,000+ in a single extreme heat event
- Heavy monsoon rains halt deliveries city-wide; worker receives zero earnings that week  
- Delhi's AQI spikes to hazardous levels; outdoor work becomes unsafe; no safety net exists
- Sudden civic unrest (strike, curfew) blocks entire neighborhoods; worker cannot access zones

**The Insurance Gap:**
- Traditional insurance products don't cover "income loss from external events"
- No insurer offers **weekly-basis** coverage matching gig workers' payout cycles  
- Claims require paperwork and investigation — but gig workers need cash *that day*
- No existing product understands their unique lifecycle

**Why This Matters:** 
A gig worker with no savings buffer cannot absorb a 20-30% weekly income loss. One bad week = missed rent, delayed food costs, debt spiral. Insurance could be life-changing.

## 💰 Weekly Premium Model

### **Why Weekly Pricing?**

Gig workers like Ravi are **paid daily or weekly** — not monthly or annually. Forcing annual insurance is misaligned with their cash flow:
- Annual premium (₹1,200–₹2,000) requires upfront capital most workers don't have
- Monthly subscription (₹200–₹300) creates fixed cost discipline they avoid
- **Weekly subscription (₹60–₹100) matches their payout cycle perfectly**

### **Tiered Plans for Ravi (Food Delivery, Motorcycle, Tier-1 City Focus)**

| **Plan** | **Weekly Premium** | **Environmental Coverage** | **Civic Coverage** | **Max Payout/Event** | **Total Yearly Commitment** | **Best For** |

| **Basic** | ₹50 | Extreme Heat (45°C+) | None | ₹250 | ₹2,600/year | Risk-averse, tight budget |

## 🚀 AI/ML Integration Strategy

### **1. AI-Powered Dynamic Premium Calculation**

**Objective:** Adjust weekly premiums based on hyper-local risk factors without manual intervention.

**Mechanism:**
- **Input Features:**
  - Historical weather patterns (last 10 years) for worker's city
  - Current seasonal forecasts (monsoon, summer, winter severity)
  - Disruption frequency in past 6 months  
  - Worker's platform activity level (high earners = different risk profile)
  - Geographic zone vulnerability (some zones have higher disruption history)

- **ML Model:** Gradient Boosting Regressor (XGBoost/LightGBM)
  - Train on historical disruption data + payout data
  - Predict weekly premium adjustment factor (0.8x–1.3x base premium)
  - Example: "Delhi summer months = 1.2x premium due to extreme heat risk"

- **Output:**
  - Worker sees: "₹75/week basic" → Adjusts to "₹90/week (summer adjustment)"
  - Transparent reasoning: "Higher risk due to upcoming heat wave season"
  - No surprise charges; worker can downgrade anytime

**Why This Matters:**
- Prevents adverse selection (healthy-season premium dumping)
- Ensures sustainability (premiums scale with actual risk)
- Builds trust (dynamic, data-driven, not arbitrary)

---

### **2. Parametric Trigger Detection (Real-Time Event API Monitoring)**

**Objective:** Detect qualifying disruptions within 15 minutes of occurrence using external APIs.

**Trigger Events & Data Sources:**

| **Disruption Type** | **Detection Source** | **Trigger Threshold** | **Verification Latency** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Extreme Heat** | OpenWeatherMap API | Temp > 44°C for 6+ hours | 5–10 minutes |
| **Heavy Rain** | OpenWeatherMap + IMD (India Met Dept) | Rainfall > 25 mm in 1 hour | 10–15 minutes |
| **Flooding** | IMD Alerts + Local Civic Authorities | Official flood warning issued | 15–20 minutes |
| **Air Pollution** | AQI APIs (WAQI, CPCB) | AQI (PM2.5) > 400 | 5–10 minutes |
| **Cyclone/Storm** | IMD Severe Weather Alert | Official alert issued | 5 minutes |
| **Curfew/Strike** | Civic Alert APIs + News feeds | Official govt closure announced | 15–30 minutes |
| **Market/Zone Closure** | Municipal Authority APIs | Delivery zone officially closed | 15–30 minutes |

**Technical Architecture:**
```
Scheduled Cron Job (Every 5 minutes)
    ↓
Poll Weather API + AQI API + Civic Alert APIs
    ↓
Check against active worker cities
    ↓
Compare to trigger thresholds
    ↓
IF event detected:
    → Fetch worker list for that city
    → Verify eligibility (active subscription, no recent payouts for same event)
    → Instantly process payout via payment gateway
    → Send SMS + Push notification to worker
    ↓
Log all events in audit trail (fraud prevention)
```

**Fraud Prevention Inside Trigger Logic:**
- Check worker's app activity logs during disruption window (must have been logged in)
- Verify worker didn't receive payout for same event earlier that day
- Cross-check against platform APIs (if possible) to confirm worker earned less that day
- Flag suspicious patterns to admin dashboard (unusual claim frequency)

---

### **3. Intelligent Fraud Detection & Claims Prevention**

**Objective:** Prevent opportunistic fraud without blocking legitimate claims.

**Fraud Detection Features:**

#### **A. Behavioral Anomaly Detection**
- **Model:** Isolation Forest or Local Outlier Factor (LOF) on payout patterns
- **Features:**
  - Frequency of claims per worker (expected: 3–5/month)
  - Timing of claims (typical: business hours, not 2 AM)
  - Geographic clustering (claims in same zone unlikely within 1 hour)
  - Platform activity correlation (did worker actually earn less that day?)

- **Output:** Risk score (0–100)
  - Score < 20: Approve instantly
  - Score 20–60: Flag for manual review; hold payout 10 minutes
  - Score > 60: Reject claim; notify worker of suspicious activity

#### **B. Real-Time Event Verification**
- Cross-check worker's location during disruption (GPS + App logs)
- Verify official disruption via 2+ independent APIs
- Query platform earnings data (if accessible) to confirm income loss
- Match claim timing with disruption onset (claims within 2 hours of event = legitimate)

#### **C. Duplicate Claim Prevention**
- Worker can only claim once per disruption event per day
- System auto-detects if worker already received payout for same weather/civic event within 24 hours
- Prevents "double dipping" on same heat wave or strike

**Example Fraud Scenario Blocked:**
```
Scammer claims "Curfew in Delhi" at 2 AM (no curfew actually announced)
→ Civic Alert API returns: No curfew found
→ Weather API returns: Normal conditions
→ System rejects claim with reason: "Event not verified by official sources"
→ Worker sees message: "Your area is not under curfew. Please try again if situation changes."
```

---

### **4. Smart Payout Strategy (Instant vs. Batched)**

**Objective:** Balance instant gratification with operational efficiency.

**Strategy:**
- **Claims < ₹500:** Process instantly (within 5 minutes) via fast payment gateway (M-Pesa, Paytm, UPI)
- **Claims ₹500–₹1,000:** Batch process within 1 hour (reduce transaction fees) 
- **Claims > ₹1,000:** Manual verification + settlement in 2 hours

**Why This Works:**
- Workers feel immediacy (most claims settle in 5 minutes)
- We reduce payment processing costs (~0.5% for batched)
- High-value claims get human eyes (fraud prevention)

## 🎯 Parametric Triggers & Claims Workflow

### **Claim Lifecycle (Fully Automated for Ravi)**

```
1. DISRUPTION OCCURS (e.g., Extreme Heat Alert)
   ↓
2. SHRAMSURAKSHA DETECTS EVENT (API monitoring)
   ↓
3. SYSTEM VERIFIES WORKER ELIGIBILITY 
   • Is Ravi's subscription active?
   • No fraud flags? No duplicate claim same day?
   ↓
4. AI ASSESSES CLAIM CREDIBILITY
   • Is external event verified by 2+ official sources?
   • Is claim timing realistic (within 30 mins of event)?
   • Does worker's activity match disruption pattern?
   ↓
5. INSTANT PAYOUT (if risk score < 20)
   • Direct wallet transfer
   • SMS notification: "₹400 received due to Heat Wave Alert"
   ↓
6. MANUAL REVIEW (if risk score 20–60)
   • Admin review within 10 minutes
   • Approve/reject with reason
   ↓
7. FRAUD REJECTION (if risk score > 60)
   • Claim denied
   • Worker notified: "Claim suspicious; contact support"
```

### **Key Constraint: LOSS OF INCOME ONLY**
- ✅ Covers: Wages lost due to inability to work
- ❌ NOT Covered: Vehicle damage, health costs, accident medical, life insurance
- ❌ NOT Covered: Routine maintenance or repairs

## 📱 Platform Strategy: Why Mobile-First?

### **Analysis: Mobile vs. Web**

Given Ravi's profile (budget Android phone, app-native usage, limited data), ShramSuraksha is **Mobile-First** (Progressive Web App + Native Android):

| Aspect | Mobile App | Web Dashboard |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Use** | Claims, Payouts, Policy View, Notifications | Admin analytics, payments verification, fraud monitoring |
| **Data Requirement** | Minimal (~2 MB) | Can use heavier assets |
| **User Base** | 95% of workers | 5% (admins, support staff) |
| **Priority** | Native Android (Phase 1), iOS (Phase 3) | Secondary |
| **Offline Capability** | Sync-on-demand (no internet = cache local state) | Real-time required |
| **Languages** | Vernacular (Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali) | English |

### **Phase 1 MVP: Mobile App (Android)**

- Policy homepage (active status, remaining coverage, next premium due)
- Claims history (past payouts received)

### **Phase 2: Web Dashboard for Admins**

### **Phase 3: iOS + Premium Features**

| Layer | Technology | Why This Choice |

| **Mobile Frontend** | React Native (Expo) | Single codebase iOS/Android; fast iteration; supports offline-first |
| **Backend API** | Node.js + Express | JavaScript ecosystem; REST API simple to ship; good libraries |
| **Database** | PostgreSQL | Relational (claims + workers); serverless via Supabase (no DevOps) |
| **AI/ML** | Python + FastAPI (separate microservice) | Industry standard; scikit-learn + pandas; isolated deployment |

| **Hosting** | Vercel (API) + Supabase (DB) + AWS (Python ML) | Serverless; no infrastructure management; scales automatically |
| **Authentication** | Firebase Auth | Phone number OTP; integrated; secure |
| **Real-time Notifications** | Firebase Cloud Messaging | Push notifications; SMS via Twilio backup |
| **Monitoring** | Sentry + LogRocket | Error tracking; performance monitoring for MVP |
